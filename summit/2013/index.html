2013 Cyber Security Summit

The Cyber Security Club at Virginia Tech is hosting our annual Cyber Security Summit, a networking event and capture the flag competition, on Saturday, April 6, 2013. We aim to connect students in the midatlantic region with an interest in cyber security.

In the morning, we will host speakers from the industry and academia to discuss the current state of cyber security as well as some innovative and exciting research in the field.

In the afternoon, we will host a capture the flag competition to give students the opportunity to hone their skills and engage in some friendly competition as they analyze and attack a network built by the officers of the Cyber Security Club. We will award points to teams that successfully compromise and retrieve flags from devices on the network, complete other challenges, or crack passwords. Points may also be awarded to teams that creatively participate in other ways.

As usual, food, drinks, and prizes for successful teams will be provided.

Getting There

The summit will be held at Virginia Tech's Arlington Research Center just outside Washington, DC. It's easy to get there by car or Metro, and easy parking is available on site.

By Car

From Washington, DC: Take I-66 West to exit 71 and turn left onto North Glebe Road. Proceed to Wilson Boulevard and take a right, take the first right onto North Wakefield Street, and take the next right. Turn left into the parking garage.

From the West: Take I-66 East to exit 71 and stay in the right lane after the first turn. At the light, turn right onto North Glebe Road, and take a right at the light onto Wilson Boulevard. Take the right onto North Wakefield Street and take the first right. Turn left into the parking garage.

By Metro

Take the Orange Line toward Vienna to the Ballston-MU Metro Station. Exit and head west on Fairfax Drive, then turn left onto North Glebe Road. The building is mid-block.
